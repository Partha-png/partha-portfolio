## the problem with coding interviews

let's be real - coding interviews can be intimidating. you're sitting there, someone's watching you code, and your brain decides to forget literally everything you've ever learned. sounds familiar? yeah, me too.

so i thought... what if there was an ai assistant that could help? not to cheat (that's lame), but to actually learn better and practice smarter.

---

## what i'm building

**fkleetcode** - an ai-assisted interview helper that:
- analyzes coding problems and breaks them down
- gives hints when you're stuck (not solutions!)
- explains concepts you're struggling with
- tracks your progress and weak areas
- simulates real interview scenarios

basically, it's like having a patient mentor who doesn't judge you for forgetting how hashmap works for the 100th time lol

---

## the tech stack

since this is an ml/ai project, here's what i'm using:

**backend:**
- python (obviously)
- langchain for the ai orchestration
- openai/gemini for the llm
- faiss for storing problem embeddings

**frontend:**
- next.js + typescript
- tailwind css (keeping it simple)
- real-time websockets for the interview simulation

---

## cool features i'm working on

### 1. smart hint system
instead of just giving you the answer, it asks questions:
- "have you considered using a different data structure?"
- "what's the time complexity of your current approach?"
- "can you optimize this nested loop?"

### 2. interview simulation mode
practice with an ai interviewer that:
- asks follow-up questions like a real interviewer
- checks your thought process, not just the code
- gives constructive feedback after

### 3. progress tracking
see where you're struggling:
- weak on dynamic programming? here are similar problems
- good at arrays but terrible at trees? focus areas highlighted
- track improvement over time

---

## challenges i'm facing

**making hints actually helpful:**
too vague = useless. too specific = basically the answer. finding the sweet spot is hard.

**avoiding the "just use chatgpt" problem:**
why use this when you can just ask chatgpt? fair point. but this is designed specifically for interview prep with structured learning paths.

**keeping latency low:**
nobody wants to wait 10 seconds for a hint during practice. optimizing the llm calls is tricky.
